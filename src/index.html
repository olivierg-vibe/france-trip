Saturday Market

Every Saturday morning through the old town — Boulevard Mirabeau and surrounding streets.

Family dates: July 4 and July 11

Timing

8:30–9:00 AM — bread and pastries before they sell out

10:00–11:00 AM — full market in swing

What to find

Fresh produce, olives, local goat cheese, honey, tapenade, lavender, Provençal fabrics, ceramics, local crafts.

Practical

Cash useful for smaller vendors. Park outside the center and walk in — no cars inside during market hours.

Wednesday Market

The main street market in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence featuring clothes, linens, and other goods.

When

Every Wednesday morning, approximately 8:00 AM – 1:00 PM

Things to Do

Near St. Rémy — Under 30 min

Les Baux-de-Provence · 20 min

Spectacular hilltop village on white limestone cliffs. Allow 2–3 hours. Go before 10 AM or late afternoon to beat July crowds.

Carrières de Lumières · 20 min · Les Baux-de-Provence

Immersive large-scale digital art projected onto the walls of a former quarry. This year: Picasso and Frida Kahlo. Book tickets in advance — sells out in July.

Saint-Paul-de-Mausole · walkable

Van Gogh's asylum. The gardens where he painted are open to visitors. Quiet and genuinely moving.

Glanum (Les Antiques) · walkable to road

Roman ruins at the southern edge of town. Triumphal arch and mausoleum visible from the road for free. The excavated site is worth an hour.

Les Alpilles Natural Park · from town

Cycling and hiking through dramatic white limestone hills. Bike rental available in town.

Day Trips — 30–60 min

Arles · 35 min

Roman amphitheater still in use, Van Gogh trail, excellent restaurant scene. Worth a full day.

Avignon · 25 min

The Palais des Papes, the Pont d'Avignon, great cafés. Easy half-day trip.

Luberon · 45–60 min

Gordes, Roussillon, Bonnieux. Plan a full day. Gordes at sunset is one of the best views in France.

Camargue · 45 min

Pink flamingos, white Camargue horses, salt flats. Best visited in the early morning.

Practical Tips

☀️ Weather

30–35°C and full sun in July. Carry water everywhere. Siesta (1–4 PM) is real — some shops close.

🇫🇷 Language

French always appreciated. English spoken in tourist contexts. Bonjour before any request goes a very long way.

💶 Cash

Useful for markets, Terre et Blé, and parking meters. Restaurants and larger shops take cards.

🍷 Rosé Wine

Provence produces exceptional rosé. Ask for something local — Domaine Hauvette, Trévallon, and Romanin are nearby estates worth knowing by name.

🚗 Driving

A car is essential from St. Rémy. Village streets are narrow. Roundabouts everywhere. Drive slowly and calmly.

🍽️ Restaurant Timing

Dinner: most restaurants open no earlier than 7:30 PM. Lunch: service typically ends by 1:30 PM. Plan accordingly.

📅 Reservations

July is peak season. Book Michelin restaurants now. Book Edú at least a week in advance.
