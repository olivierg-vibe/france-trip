## St. Rémy-de-Provence Guide

### Restaurants

**Fine Dining**

- **L'Auberge de Saint-Rémy** — Michelin-starred, led by Chef Fanny
  Rey. The benchmark for a special-occasion dinner in town.
  Reserve well in advance for July.

- **Restaurant de Tourrel** — Michelin-starred, set in a beautifully
  restored 17th-century mansion. Tasting menus and exceptional wine
  list. Book early.

- **Domaine de Baumanière** (20 min away, Les Baux-de-Provence) — Two
  Michelin stars. One of the landmark addresses in French gastronomy.
  Worth the drive for a truly special evening. Dress accordingly.

**Casual and Local Favorites**

- **Edú** — 17 Av. Albert Schweitzer, 13210 Saint-Rémy-de-Provence
  Phone: +33 4 90 20 94 21 | edu-restaurant.com
  Set in a former plant nursery with a terrace around a pond.
  Mediterranean plates for sharing, natural wines, superb Provençal
  atmosphere. Fooding and Gault & Millau listed. Reservations
  recommended. CLOSED Sunday and Monday. Open Tuesday to Saturday,
  lunch (noon-1:30 PM) and dinner (7-9 PM). Own parking on site.

- **Olga by le Bistrot Découverte** — Beloved local bistro named after
  the grandmother who cooked for love. Consistently excellent.
  TheFork rating 8.9.

- **Restaurant Lou Patio** (Hotel le Saint-Rémy) — Garden terrace
  dining. TheFork rating 8.9.

- **Bistrot Canto Cigalo** (Le Vallon de Valrugues) — Classic Provençal
  setting in a beautiful hotel. TheFork rating 8.6.

- **Bienbon** — Creative vegetarian and vegan-friendly menu. Useful
  for diverse dietary needs in the group.

- **Basta Cosi!** — Casual Italian for a relaxed evening.

### The Saturday Market
One of the finest markets in Provence. Every Saturday morning through
the old town — Boulevard Mirabeau and surrounding streets.
The family will be there for July 5 and July 11.

- Arrive 8:30-9 AM for bread and pastries before they sell out.
  10-11 AM for the full market in swing.
- Find: fresh produce, olives, local goat cheese, honey, tapenade,
  lavender, Provençal fabrics, ceramics, local crafts.
- Practical: cash useful for smaller vendors. Park outside the center
  and walk in — no cars inside during market hours.

---

### Things to Do

**Near St. Rémy (under 30 min)**

- **Les Baux-de-Provence** — 20-min drive. Spectacular hilltop village
  perched on white limestone cliffs. Allow 2-3 hours minimum. Go
  before 10 AM or late afternoon to beat July crowds.

- **Saint-Paul-de-Mausole** — Van Gogh's asylum, walkable from the
  center of St. Rémy. The gardens where he painted are open to
  visitors. Quiet and genuinely moving.

- **Glanum (Les Antiques)** — Roman ruins at the southern edge of
  town. The triumphal arch and mausoleum are visible from the road
  for free. The excavated site is worth an hour.

- **Les Alpilles Natural Park** — Cycling and hiking through dramatic
  white limestone hills. Bike rental available in town.

**Day Trips (30-60 min)**

- **Arles** — 35 min. Roman amphitheater still in use, Van Gogh
  trail, excellent restaurant scene. Worth a full day.

- **Avignon** — 25 min. The Palais des Papes, the Pont d'Avignon,
  great cafés. Easy half-day trip.

- **Luberon** — Gordes, Roussillon, Bonnieux. Plan a full day.
  Gordes at sunset is one of the best views in France.

- **Camargue** — 45 min. Pink flamingos, white Camargue horses,
  salt flats. Best visited in the early morning.

### Practical Tips

- Weather: 30-35°C and full sun in July. Carry water everywhere.
  Siesta (1-4 PM) is real — some shops close.
- Language: French always appreciated. English spoken in tourist
  contexts. Bonjour before any request goes a very long way.
- Cash: Useful for markets, Terre et Blé, and parking meters.
  Restaurants and larger shops take cards.
- Rosé wine: Provence produces exceptional rosé. Ask for something
  local — Domaine Hauvette, Trévallon, and Romanin are nearby
  estates worth knowing by name.
- Driving: A car is essential from St. Rémy. Village streets are
  narrow. Roundabouts everywhere. Drive slowly and calmly.
- Restaurant timing: Most don't open for dinner before 7:30 PM.
  Lunch service typically ends by 1:30 PM. Plan accordingly.
- Reservations: July is peak season. Book Michelin restaurants now.
  Book Edú at least a week in advance.
